Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) 最大似然估计

Assume we get a probability function pX(x;θ)p_X(x; \theta) with unconfirmed parameters, θ\theta . Additionally, there is a data set x={x1,x2,…,xn}x=\{x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n\} , then the MLE of θ\theta is :

θ̂ML=arg⁡max⁡θpX(x1,x2,…,xn;θ)=arg⁡max⁡θlog⁡pX(x1,x2,…,xn;θ) \begin{aligned} \hat{\theta}_{\mathrm{ML}} &= \arg\max_{\theta} p_{X}(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n; \theta) \\ &=\arg\max_{\theta} \log p_{X}(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n; \theta) \end{aligned}

if x1,x2,…,xnx_1, x_2, \dots, x_n are independent, then:

θ̂ML=arg⁡max⁡θ∏i=1npX(xi;θ)=arg⁡max⁡θ∑i=1nlog⁡pX(xi;θ) \begin{aligned} \hat{\theta}_{\mathrm{ML}} &= \arg\max_{\theta} \prod_{i=1}^n p_{X}(x_i; \theta) \\ &= \arg\max_{\theta} \sum_{i=1}^n \log p_{X}(x_i; \theta) \end{aligned}

MLE can be biased （最大似然估计不一定是无偏的）

Consistency

θ̂ML→pθ\hat\theta_{\mathrm{ML}}\xrightarrow{p} \theta as n→∞n\rightarrow \infty . We say Xn→pXX_n \xrightarrow{p} X as n→∞n\rightarrow \infty if

lim⁡n→Pr(∥Xn−X∥>ϵ)=0,∀ϵ∈ℝ \lim_{n\rightarrow}\mathrm{Pr}(\|X_n-X\|>\epsilon) = 0, \forall \epsilon\in \mathbb{R}

we have n(θ̂ML−θ)→d𝒩(0,I(θ)−1)\sqrt{n} \left(\hat\theta_{\mathrm{ML}} - \theta\right)\xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}(0, I(\theta)^{-1}) as n→∞n\rightarrow \infty, where I(θ)I(\theta) is fisher information of θ\theta (larger information ⇒\Longrightarrow smaller variance)

We say Xn→dXX_n\xrightarrow{d}X as n→∞n\rightarrow \infty if

Central Limit Theorem (CLT) 中心极限定理

Suppose X1,X2,X3,…X_1, X_2, X_3, \dots is a sequence of i.i.d random variables with 𝔼[Xi]=μ\mathbb{E}[X_i]=\mu and var[Xi]=σ<∞\mathrm{var}[X_i]=\sigma<\infty . then as n→∞n\rightarrow\infty , the distribution of n(X¯n−μ)\sqrt{n}(\overline{X}_n-\mu) converges to 𝒩(0,σ2)\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2) .

This implies that, when σ>0\sigma>0, the CDF of n(X¯n−μ)\sqrt{n}(\overline{X}_n-\mu) converge pointwise to the CDF of standard normal CDF, which is :

lim⁡n→∞Pr(n(X¯n−μ)≤z)=lim⁡n→∞Pr(n(X¯n−μ)σ≤zσ)=Φ(zσ) \lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} \mathrm{Pr}(\sqrt{n}(\overline{X}_n-\mu)\leq z)=\lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} \mathrm{Pr}\left(\frac{\sqrt{n}(\overline{X}_n-\mu)}{\sigma}\leq \frac{z}{\sigma}\right)=\Phi\left(\frac{z}{\sigma}\right)
